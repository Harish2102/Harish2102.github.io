Harish Balasubramanian

Biomedical Imaging × AI × Embedded Systems

I am a biomedical engineer who likes to stay close to the hardware. Most of my work lives where instrumentation meets machine learning: I build the imaging tools that capture biological data, then design the models that turn that data into something a researcher or clinician can actually use. What I care about is the whole path, from a raw signal or a microscopy frame all the way through to a result that holds up when someone depends on it, and I want to keep working across that full stack rather than inside a single silo.

I am an M.S.E. Biomedical Engineering candidate at Johns Hopkins University (GPA 3.87) and a Research Assistant in the Pathak Lab at the School of Medicine. I am currently an AI Intern at HDMedical, bringing hospital-grade cardiac screening to handheld devices.
